📈 Portfolio Signals

signals.html — BUY / HOLD / SELL on your portfolio & watchlist

  • Signal = shortlist only, not an auto-trade. Act when 2 of 3 confirming factors align: MACD bullish, RSI 40–65, Volume >1.2×.
  • Prefer entries at RSI 40–58 — that's room to run before overbought, not already extended.
  • Ignore a BUY if MACD is still bearish — that's oversold, not confirmed reversal yet.
  • On SELL: stop adding to the position immediately, and exit promptly if volume is above 2× average.
  • Strong BUY + 2 confirming factors = full position size. Moderate BUY + 1 factor = half size, add on next run's confirmation. Moderate BUY alone = watchlist only, don't enter yet.
  • Never average down into a SELL signal — the score doesn't care what price you originally paid.

🎰 Long Calls

long-calls.html — bullish bets on names with a live BUY signal, grouped by timeframe

Weekly — 7–14 days to expiry
  • Fast, high-gamma trades — only for a setup you expect to resolve in days, not weeks.
  • Cap risk at ~1% of capital per position — theta decay accelerates hard in the final week.
  • Take profit at +50–100%; don't wait for more, weeklies give it back fast on a stall.
  • Hard exit 2–3 days before expiry regardless of P&L to dodge the final theta cliff.
  • Best paired with a live BUY signal or a confirmed near-term catalyst, not a hope trade.
Monthly — 30–60 days to expiry
  • The standard swing-trade runway — enough time for a thesis to play out without crushing theta.
  • Size at ~2–3% of capital per position; scale out in tiers at +50%, +75%, +100%.
  • Reassess if the underlying's signal flips to HOLD or SELL — the option thesis just broke.
  • Roll to the next month if the move is working but you're inside the last 2 weeks.
LEAPS — 90–180 days to expiry
  • Behaves like a leveraged stock substitute — use it for names you'd be happy to hold outright.
  • Can size larger, up to ~5% of capital, since theta decay is much gentler this far out.
  • Revisit and consider rolling forward once you're inside the last 45–60 days.
  • Best candidates are portfolio/watchlist names already on your radar, not lottery tickets.
GENERAL RISK RULES
  • Keep total capital tied up across ALL open long-call positions under ~10–15% of your account.
  • Always use limit orders — option spreads can be wide; market orders overpay on entry and underpay on exit.
  • High IV means you're paying more time value — be extra disciplined on profit-taking, don't let it round-trip.
  • Set an expiry reminder for every contract. An option expiring worthless because you forgot is the most avoidable way to lose 100% of a position.
  • Score is a liquidity/affordability/upside/IV composite, not a prediction — it tells you the option is well-structured, not that the stock will move.

🎯 Covered Calls

covered-calls.html — income against portfolio positions already at BUY

  • You need 100 shares of the underlying per contract sold — this only applies to positions you already hold (or are opening).
  • Selling the call caps your upside at the strike — if the stock rips past it, you get called away and miss the rest of the move. That's the trade-off for the premium income.
  • Pick a strike you're genuinely OK selling at. If you'd be upset to lose the shares at that price, go further OTM even for less premium.
  • If the stock approaches the strike before expiry and you want to keep your shares, roll the call out (and often up) rather than letting it get exercised.
  • Check the ex-dividend date if the stock pays one — early assignment risk goes up right before it.
  • Annualized yield lets you compare across different expiries fairly; the raw "Yield (this exp.)" is what you actually collect if held to that expiry.

⚖️ Position Sizing Across All Three

How the strategies fit together as one account, not three separate bets

  • These aren't three separate accounts — they draw on the same capital. Total long-call exposure (Weekly + Monthly + LEAPS combined) should stay under ~10–15% of your account at any time.
  • Covered calls don't need new capital — they're income against shares you already hold, so they don't compete with long-call sizing at all. Treat them as a separate, parallel income stream.
  • Signal strength should gate size everywhere: a Strong BUY with 2+ confirming factors supports a full-size stock entry AND is the best candidate for a long call. A Moderate BUY with only 1 factor is watchlist-only for stock, and skip it entirely for a long call.
  • If a long-call underlying flips to SELL after you're in the trade, that's an exit signal for the option — don't wait for the option's own price action to tell you, the stock signal already did.
  • Covered calls and long calls on the SAME ticker at the same time only makes sense if they're at different strikes/expiries with a clear reason (e.g. selling calls above your cost basis on shares you'll hold long-term, while separately buying a call to add short-term upside) — otherwise you're just paying commissions to offset yourself.
