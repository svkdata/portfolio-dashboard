HOW TO PLAY CASH-SECURED PUTS
  • This is the entry point when you DON'T have 100 shares yet — no stock purchase required upfront, just cash held aside.
  • You need the full collateral (strike × 100) sitting in the account as cash — that's what "cash-secured" means.
  • Two outcomes, both fine: stock stays above your strike → the put expires worthless and you keep 100% of the premium, no shares bought. Stock drops below your strike → you get assigned and buy 100 shares AT your strike, usually below today's price.
  • If assigned, you now own the shares — that's exactly when the Covered Calls tab becomes usable on that ticker. Sell calls against the new position going forward ("the wheel").
  • Only sell puts on names you'd genuinely be OK owning at that strike — this only makes sense on tickers you already want, not random high-premium names.
  • Est. delta is an approximation (Black-Scholes from implied vol, since option data providers don't give real Greeks here) — treat it as a rough "how likely to get assigned" signal, not a precise number.
