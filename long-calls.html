Weekly

7–14 days to expiry · 4 candidate(s)
HOW TO PLAY WEEKLY
  • Fast, high-gamma trades — only for a setup you expect to resolve in days, not weeks.
  • Cap risk at ~1% of capital per position — theta decay accelerates hard in the final week.
  • Take profit at +50–100%; don't wait for more, weeklies give it back fast on a stall.
  • Hard exit 2–3 days before expiry regardless of P&L to dodge the final theta cliff.
  • Best paired with a live BUY signal or a confirmed near-term catalyst, not a hope trade.

Monthly

30–60 days to expiry · 7 candidate(s)
HOW TO PLAY MONTHLY
  • The standard swing-trade runway — enough time for a thesis to play out without crushing theta.
  • Size at ~2–3% of capital per position; scale out in tiers at +50%, +75%, +100%.
  • Reassess if the underlying's signal flips to HOLD or SELL — the option thesis just broke.
  • Roll to the next month if the move is working but you're inside the last 2 weeks.

HOW TO PLAY LEAPS
  • Behaves like a leveraged stock substitute — use it for names you'd be happy to hold outright.
  • Can size larger, up to ~5% of capital, since theta decay is much gentler this far out.

GENERAL RISK RULES
  • Keep total capital tied up across ALL open long-call positions under ~10–15% of your account.
  • Always use limit orders — option spreads can be wide; market orders overpay on entry and underpay on exit.
  • High IV means you're paying more time value — be extra disciplined on profit-taking, don't let it round-trip.
  • Set an expiry reminder for every contract. An option expiring worthless because you forgot is the most avoidable way to lose 100% of a position.
  • Score is a liquidity/affordability/upside/IV composite, not a prediction — it tells you the option is well-structured, not that the stock will move.

Not financial advice. Options can expire worthless — size accordingly.
